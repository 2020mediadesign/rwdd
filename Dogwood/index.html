North Carolina Drought Tolerant Plants.

Choosing plants is one of the most important ports of a landscape project. The plants should be compatible with the landscape design and well suited to the environment, which includes the soils and light exposure. For a wise-water-use landscape, also known as a xeriscape, the waterneeds of plants are also important.

Xeriscaping involves not only plant selection but also grouping plants according to water needs. If plants are grouped properly, supplemental irrigation can be applied only to the plants that need it. To help with grouping, this listing of plants includes water-use zones for eoch plant. The water-use zones are divided into three categories -

(1) regulor irrigation;

(2) moderate, occassional irrigation; and

(3) low, no irrigation.

This information is based on observation, experience, and several cross references from notable horticultural sources. Many plants can grow in any or all of the three zones. For the greatest water conservation, use plants in zones that require the least amount of water.

The conditions of the planting area should match the water-use zone. Place plants that require more water in areas of the landscape that stay moist and plants that need less in areas that are naturally drier.

It is surprising to learn how many plants can thrive without supplemental water once they are established.
